## The day

This is a proper good day to go sailing. Wall-to-wall sunshine, comfortable air in the mid-70s, and a friendly working breeze in the 9–10 kt range that firms up a touch as the afternoon goes on. It's a fun, spirited daysail rather than a lazy drifter — enough wind to keep the boat heeled and moving without ever feeling like a handful. One honest caveat: the models don't fully agree on strength (one is notably lighter), so if it comes in softer than expected it'll just be an easy, mellow ghost along instead. Either way, you'll have a nice time.

## Wind

The breeze is out of the **northeast** first thing (around 30°) and slowly **veers toward the east** through the day, ending up more easterly (60–90°) by late afternoon. This is a gradient wind off the land rather than the classic afternoon sea breeze, so expect it to be a bit **shifty and puffy** — steady averages around 9–10 kt with gusts poking into the **15–17 kt** range, a little stronger out toward the harbor mouth and **south of Hog Island** than up inside the harbor.

**Best window: early-to-mid afternoon**, when it's warmest and the breeze is at its fullest and most reliable. Being a northeasterly, the softest patches will sit **tucked up under the shores to the north** and in the wind shadow off the land — if you drift into a hole near the top of the harbor or close under a shoreline, nose back out toward open water and the pressure fills back in. It'll be a touch more consistent out toward **Hog Island and the East Passage**.

*(Note: the Newport and Conimicut live anemometers weren't reporting when this was put together, so the wind read is from the forecast models.)*

## Tide & current

- **Low water ~10:42 (just 0.7 ft)** — right at the start of the window, so the harbor edges and shoals will be at their skinniest mid-morning. Give the shallow shoreline spots and the flats a wide berth if you're heading out early.
- **High water 18:06 (3.4 ft)** — the tide floods in all afternoon, so there's plenty of water under you as the day goes on. Ideal timing.

Current is gentle around Bristol: near-slack at the **harbor mouth around 11:00**, then a light flood (into the harbor, ~0.3 kt) building through the afternoon. Out in the **East Passage** it runs a bit stronger (up to ~0.7 kt on the afternoon flood). Nothing dramatic — but with the wind having veered east against the afternoon's up-bay flood, you may notice a little **short chop in the harbor mouth and Passage** later in the day. It's a heads-up, not a warning; it'll be bouncy rather than rough.

## Good to know

Beautiful summer conditions: **sunny and clear all day, 73–78°F, no rain in the forecast** and no thunderstorm risk. Bring water, sunscreen, and a hat — that clear sky means full sun on the water for hours. It'll feel warm ashore but breezier and cooler out sailing, so a light layer is handy.

With gusts into the mid-teens and a puffy, shifting northeasterly, this is a **life-jackets-on kind of day**, especially for kids or less experienced crew — nothing scary, just enough that a gust can catch you leaning. A nice plan: **head out late morning or early afternoon** once the low tide has passed and there's more water under you, ride the best of the breeze, and aim to be **back before 6 pm** as the day winds down. Enjoy it — days like this are why we keep boats in Bristol.
