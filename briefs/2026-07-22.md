## Tactical call
**Course-agnostic:** Two levers point the same way tonight — **pressure and current both live to the S/seaward.** The breeze is best S of Hog Island and in the East Passage, and it's softening, so getting to that pressure matters more than a small shift play. The **ebb sets SSW seaward all window**, so:
- Heading **seaward (S)** you're pushed along — favour it.
- Heading **up-bay (N)** you're punching the ebb; in the East Passage the ~0.9 kt mid-channel set is the biggest single loss on the course, so **work the shallower shore edges (stay out of the channel axis)** when going N.
- Distrust the **Poppasquash (west shore) lee** — light and swinging (backing toward S); don't get parked there chasing a shift.

**If the course is set:**
- **N–S windward/leeward:** the E–W gradient is modest; the decider is N/S current — bank the ebb on the runs seaward, minimize it on the beats up-bay by edging out of the channel.
- **J-hook out of the harbor:** near-slack, shifty start inside, then you hook into a strengthening SSW ebb at the mouth — the current flips from non-issue to first-order once you clear the gut; play each leg on its own.
- **Around Hog Island:** every leg gets its own call — SSW ebb helps the seaward/southbound legs and hurts the northbound; strongest flow through the entrance gut and off the island tips.
- **Out-and-back (light air):** pressure dominates — get S to the East Passage breeze; the ebb gives a free ride out and a grind back, so leave margin for the slower return.
