Bristol Harbor · Rhode Island

Wed 22 Jul 2026 · 18:00–20:00

Race window 18:00–20:00

Easing SSW evening sea breeze (6–7 kt, gusts ~20), ebb running seaward the whole window and building toward peak ~18:30 — plus a falling tide toward a 0.99 ft low.

Wind

Light-to-moderate SSW sea breeze filling up the East Passage: race-window averages sit 6.3–7.4 kt across the fixes with gusts to ~20 kt, so it's a puffy, patchy evening rather than a solid pressure. Direction is SSW, roughly 200–210° at the seaward fixes (Hog mouth, S of Hog, East Passage), where it holds steady. Inside it's less settled — the inner harbor backs 220°→187° and off Poppasquash Point it backs hard 238°→176°, the classic soft, veer-and-back patch in the Neck's lee.

Fix gradient favours the seaward end: most breeze is S of Hog Island (7.4 kt) and out in the East Passage (6.7 kt), versus 6.3–6.6 kt inside. Live Conimicut Light (~3 mi N up the Passage) read 18 kt from the S at 13:42 — a healthy afternoon sea breeze that the forecast has easing into the 18:00–20:00 evening window, so expect softening and hunt for the pressure lanes to the S/seaward.

Current

Ebb runs the entire race window, setting SSW down-bay / seaward: - Harbor mouth (Hog Island NW): ebb builds from ~18:00, peaks ~0.3 kt at 18:47 on ~199°, next slack not until 20:42 — after the finish. - East Passage (Dyer Island W): materially stronger — ebb ~0.9 kt on ~216°, peaking ~18:17, slack not until 21:54.

So the seaward current only strengthens through the race, and there's no mid-race reversal — it's ebb start to finish.

Tide

Falling all window: from H 3.54 ft at 15:13 down toward L 0.99 ft at 21:51 (just after the finish). Water is dropping through the evening — mind depth over shallow marks and along the harbor edges late in the race, and check committee-boat depth near the harbor mouth.

Tactical call

Course-agnostic: Two levers point the same way tonight — pressure and current both live to the S/seaward. The breeze is best S of Hog Island and in the East Passage, and it's softening, so getting to that pressure matters more than a small shift play. The ebb sets SSW seaward all window, so: - Heading seaward (S) you're pushed along — favour it. - Heading up-bay (N) you're punching the ebb; in the East Passage the ~0.9 kt mid-channel set is the biggest single loss on the course, so work the shallower shore edges (stay out of the channel axis) when going N. - Distrust the Poppasquash (west shore) lee — light and swinging (backing toward S); don't get parked there chasing a shift.

If the course is set: - N–S windward/leeward: the E–W gradient is modest; the decider is N/S current — bank the ebb on the runs seaward, minimize it on the beats up-bay by edging out of the channel. - J-hook out of the harbor: near-slack, shifty start inside, then you hook into a strengthening SSW ebb at the mouth — the current flips from non-issue to first-order once you clear the gut; play each leg on its own. - Around Hog Island: every leg gets its own call — SSW ebb helps the seaward/southbound legs and hurts the northbound; strongest flow through the entrance gut and off the island tips. - Out-and-back (light air): pressure dominates — get S to the East Passage breeze; the ebb gives a free ride out and a grind back, so leave margin for the slower return.
